# Customer Discovery

## Goal

Step 1 is not random outreach.
It is testing a small number of sharp buyer hypotheses and listening for where the pain is strongest.

## Buckets To Test

### 1. Legal and compliance-heavy services

Question:

Can we make your SOP-heavy review work executable?

### 2. Recruiting and staffing teams

Question:

Can we run repeatable judgment-heavy workflows without losing human control?

### 3. Insurance / claims support teams

Question:

Can we combine SOPs, escalation, and agent assistance in a controlled workflow?

### 4. Internal operations teams

Question:

Can we turn messy team process into repeatable supervised execution?

## Interview Questions

Ask the same 3 core questions each time:

1. Walk me through a recent workflow that went wrong or got stuck.
2. Where do people rely on docs, memory, or manager review?
3. If this worked, what outcome would matter most?

## Good Signals

- they already have SOPs/checklists/docs
- the workflow repeats often
- mistakes are expensive
- human review is normal
- they react strongly without much education

## Bad Signals

- they mostly ask for integrations
- they want full autonomy with no review
- the workflow is too bespoke
- they cannot name one painful recurring process

## Scorecard

Score each segment on:

- pain intensity
- workflow frequency
- willingness to pilot
- budget ownership
- speed to decision
- ease of distribution

## Expected Output

Customer discovery should produce:

- one best industry bucket
- one painful recurring workflow
- one buyer title
- one repeatable acquisition channel
