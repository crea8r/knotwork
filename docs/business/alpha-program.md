# Alpha Program

## Goal

Use the alpha to validate:

- market pull
- workflow fit
- willingness to onboard real work
- which positioning line gets the strongest reaction

## Alpha Candidate Profile

Best early candidates:

- 20-150 employee teams
- English-speaking
- repeatable knowledge-heavy workflows
- one clear workflow owner
- willing to test on real work, not just give opinions

## What To Ask From Alpha Users

- one real workflow to map
- one operator point of contact
- permission to observe breakdowns and edge cases
- feedback on language, not just product behavior

## What To Promise

- direct onboarding help
- rapid iteration on one workflow
- priority support
- influence over product direction

Do not promise:

- broad connector coverage
- enterprise hardening
- production-grade guarantees you do not have

## Alpha Success Criteria

Good alpha outcomes:

- workflow is used repeatedly
- team trusts escalation and resume behavior
- users describe the value in their own words
- they ask to keep using it after the test

## Alpha Failure Signals

- users treat it as a demo only
- they compare only on integrations
- they cannot identify a workflow to run
- they like the idea but not enough to change behavior
