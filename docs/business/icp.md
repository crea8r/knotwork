# ICP

## Best First Customer Profile

A good first customer for Knotwork is:

- a 20-300 person company
- in a service or risk-sensitive industry
- with teams doing repeatable, judgment-heavy workflows
- where people currently work from SOP docs, checklists, templates, and manager review
- where mistakes or inconsistency are expensive

## Priority Industry Buckets

### 1. Legal and compliance-heavy services

Why:

- strong need for human review
- high value per workflow
- SOPs already exist in docs
- auditability is easy to sell

Likely buyers:

- founder
- practice lead
- head of operations
- compliance lead

### 2. Recruiting and staffing

Why:

- repeated workflows
- lots of semi-structured judgment
- easier to demo
- likely faster sales cycle than legal

Likely buyers:

- founder
- recruiting operations lead
- delivery manager

### 3. Insurance / claims / underwriting support

Why:

- supervised workflows are normal
- escalation is expected
- knowledge-heavy work
- good long-term fit

Likely buyers:

- operations lead
- claims manager
- risk operations manager

### 4. Internal support / operations teams

Why:

- broad market
- clear review and triage pain
- easier to reach than some regulated verticals

Risk:

- may pull Knotwork toward generic automation comparisons

## Lower-Priority Buyers

- developer tools companies
- IT automation teams
- RevOps teams optimizing app sync
- enterprises demanding a huge connector catalog on day 1

These buyers tend to compare everything to:

- n8n
- Zapier
- Workato
- internal tooling

## Geography

### Primary

- United States
- United Kingdom
- Australia
- Canada

Why:

- English-language SOPs are common
- labor costs are high enough that workflow efficiency matters
- AI tooling adoption is stronger

### Secondary

- Singapore
- UAE

### Lower Priority At First

- most of continental Europe

Reason:

- more language fragmentation
- more localization burden
- slower go-to-market for a small team

## Best Initial Wedge

If a single narrow wedge is needed, start with:

- US or UK
- legal services, compliance services, or recruiting firms
- 20-150 employees
- first workflow: intake -> review -> escalate -> approve
